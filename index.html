Role of The Gameplay Programmer

Feb 20th, 2021

What is a Gameplay Programmer?

A Gameplay Programmer is the person responsible for actually constructing the game, for realising the design. They typically are responisble for the 3Cs; Character, Camera and Controls. It is a versatile role however that can often involve the programmer touching on a range of other aspects and systems such as UI, AI, audio and more.

Whose Hiring?

Ubisoft - Malmo, Sweden

Ubisoft are looking for a gameplay programmer for work on an untitled Star Wars project. This exciting position specifies that the programmer will be working on player centric features such as movements, controls, combat and camera.

It is expected of the programmer that they solve problems and fix bugs, make use of modern techniques in there work, and have an awareness for performance critical programming which is essential for the scale of game they will be working on. The applicant should have professional experience in this role, and have shipped at least one full game before so that they are aware of the process and life-cycle.

Rebellion - Oxford, England

Rebellion are looking for someone to help work on an unannounced title for Playstation 4, Xbox One and PC. It is a versatile role that will involve developing features such as strategy, mechanics, logic, combat systems, AI, UI, audio code and more. Furthermore, they are looking for someone who will maintain and debug their code, mainitain techncial documentation of their work and ensure that they optimise their code where they can for better performance.

It is a full-time role that asks for a strong academic background with a preferation for a 1st class or 2:1 degree in a computer science or related degree. 1-2 years of professional C++ experience in games is required.

Frontier Developments - Cambridge, England

Frontier Development is looking for experienced gameplay programmers on an unannounced project. They will work with the whole game team to bring all the design, code and assets together. Individuals will gain a deep understanding of game design and its implementation.

It is essential that applicants have had experience in a programming role on at least one commercially released game and that they have a strong understanding and knowledge of C++. It is also important that the individual can work well in a team, working within a component-based game object / entity based architecture.

What Is Required To Get a Job In This Field?

From this it seems to be able to get a job in this field usually requires one to have obtained a bachlor's degree relevant to game design and a good few years of experience. It is also important that the programmer exhibits excellent proficiency in C++ to a professional level. Having a strong passion and motivation as well as a great interest in playing video games is essential to succeeding in this field.

Similiar Roles and Salary

Interestingly as shown in the figures below, salaries for a game programmer can range from around $40,000 to $100,000. For the technical director howeever the lower end could be earning around $100,000 but on the higher end reaching $250,000! Experience of course plays a big part, as seen below a programmer with more experience will earn more than where they began. With this experience they could even advance to a higher position in their field such as a lead, who will typically earn more.

Source: https://www.gameindustrycareerguide.com/video-game-programmer-salary/

Advice For Aspiring Gameplay Programmers

  • Make as many games as you can. You will only get better the more you make.
  • Take part in any and all game jams or contests you come across.
  • Have an awareness of the different systems i.e. if you are a playstation gamer, play on an xbox or try out PC.
  • If your maths skills are lacking, brush up on them.
  • Always be learning. Regularly read up on what you don't know, strengthen your weaknesss and sharpen your strengths.
  • Have a strong passion for this career path, otherwise this role may not be for you.
